# Rollcall: User System Guide & Experience Overview

Welcome to Rollcall! This guide explains how the platform works from start to finish, helping you manage your community, track attendance, and celebrate milestones with ease.

---

## 1. Getting Started: The Setup
Everything in Rollcall starts with an **Organization** (this could be your church, school, or social club).

### Use Case A: For Organization Creators (Owners)
If you are the primary leader, you'll start by creating your Organization. 
1. **Register**: Sign up as an administrator.
2. **Setup**: Name your organization (e.g., "St. Peter’s Bolton").
3. **The Dashboard**: Once created, you have the "Master Key" to the organization. You can manage everything, including settings and deletion.

### Use Case B: For Joining an Existing Organization
If your church/club already uses Rollcall:
1. **Discover**: Search for your organization's name in the "Discover" section.
2. **Request to Join**: Send a request to the owner to be added as an admin.
3. **Approval**: Once the owner approves you, you’ll see the organization's units on your dashboard.

---

## 2. Organizing Your Community: Units
Organizations are big, so Rollcall breaks them down into **Units**.
* **Examples**: "Youth Choir," "Men’s Fellowship," "Bible Study Group," or "Sunday School."

### Use Case: Managing a Unit
As an admin, you can create a Unit to manage your specific department.
* **Distributed Responsibility**: If you create a unit, *you* are responsible for it. The organization owner can see it, but you manage the day-to-day work. This keeps things organized and prevents any one person from being overwhelmed.

---

## 3. Managing People: Members
Once your Unit is ready, it’s time to add your people.

### Use Case: Keeping Your Records Up-to-Date
* **Individual Entry**: Add members one by one with their name, phone number, and section.
* **CSV Import**: Have a long list already? You can upload a spreadsheet to add everyone at once.
* **Birthday Tracking**: When adding a member, include their birthday. Rollcall will automatically notify you a day before and on their special day so you can reach out!

---

## 4. The Main Event: Services & Attendance
The heart of Rollcall is tracking who shows up.

### Use Case: Running a Service
1. **Create a Service**: Give it a name (e.g., "Sunday Service" or "Weekly Rehearsal").
2. **The Signature Check-in Link**: Rollcall generates a unique link for every service. 
3. **Share or Scan**: You can share this link in your WhatsApp group, post it on social media, or display it as a QR code at your venue.

### Use Case: The Member Experience (Check-in)
* **One-Click attendance**: Members click the link on their phones.
* **Search & Confirm**: They search for their name, click it, and they are marked as "Present."
* **Real-time Updates**: On your Admin Dashboard, the headcount updates live as people check in. You see exactly who is in the room as it happens.

---

## 5. Staying Engaged: Notifications & Badges
Rollcall isn't just about data; it's about people.

### Use Case: The Birthday Notification
* **The Bell Icon**: If a member has a birthday, a notification bell will appear on your dashboard.
* **Badge of Honor**: On a member's birthday, a special cake icon appears next to their name in your lists, so you can give them a warm welcome when you see them.

---

## 6. Summary of Flow
1. **Admin** sets up or joins an **Organization**.
2. **Units** are created for different departments.
3. **Members** are added to these Units.
4. **Services** are scheduled when events happen.
5. **Check-in Links** are shared with members.
6. **Attendance** is tracked live, and **Birthdays** are celebrated as they arise.

---

**Rollcall ensures you spend less time on paperwork and more time building your community.**
