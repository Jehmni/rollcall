# Design Philosophy: Monochrome First

## Core Principle: "Greyscale is the interface. Colour is the signal."

The Yevo product operates in a restrained palette of Charcoal, Grey, and Off-white. This creates clarity, focus, and trust. Nothing competes for attention. Every screen represents people and presence; noise is the enemy of awareness.

## 1. The Monochrome Foundation
The interface stays quiet, so the moments that matter stand out.
*   **Charcoal:** `#131313` (Base backgrounds, deep architectural presence)
*   **Grey:** `#757575` / `#BFBFBF` (Secondary text, metadata, structural "ghost" borders)
*   **Off-white:** `#F5F5F5` (Primary text, active content)

## 2. The Signal Palette (Meaning Only)
Your brand colours are not used for styling. They are used for meaning.
*   **Signal Amber (#F0A500):** Active attention, ongoing processes, key metrics (e.g., attendance ring). Appears when something needs to be noticed.
*   **Found Teal (#00C9A7):** Confirmed presence, successful return, positive state. Appears when someone is accounted for.
*   **Functional Red (#EF5350):** Absence, failed check-in, critical action. Appears only when something requires intervention.

## 3. Visual Restrictions (Non-Negotiable)
*   **No Coloured Buttons:** Buttons must be monochrome (Off-white background with charcoal text, or Ghost Grey).
*   **No Decorative Accents:** We remove decoration to reveal what matters.
*   **No Gradients:** Use flat, solid tones for architectural clarity.
*   **No "Brand Colour everywhere":** If everything is coloured, nothing matters.

## 4. Typography & Shape
*   **Headlines:** Newsreader (Editorial Serif) for authoritative, archival presence.
*   **Body/Data:** Inter (Technical Sans) for functional clarity.
*   **Shapes:** Hard, sharp edges (**0px border radius**) for a high-end infrastructure aesthetic.

## 5. Result
The interface stays calm, decisions become obvious, and signals become unmistakable.
one-line summary: **Monochrome by default. Colour when it counts.**
